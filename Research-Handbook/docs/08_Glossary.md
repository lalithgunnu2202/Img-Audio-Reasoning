# Project Glossary



## 1. Key Definitions

| Term | Domain | Definition |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Environmental Audio Reasoning (EAR)** | Core Theme | The process of parsing visual information to infer the corresponding physical, material, and spatial conditions of an environment, and using this model to predict the realistic soundscape. |
| **Acoustic Impedance** | Acoustics | A measure of the resistance a material presents to the flow of acoustic waves. Higher impedance surfaces reflect more sound, whereas lower impedance surfaces absorb it. |
| **Head-Related Transfer Function (HRTF)** | Spatial Audio | A response function that characterizes how an ear receives sound from a specific point in space, accounting for reflections from the listener's head, shoulders, and pinnae to enable binaural 3D hearing. |
| **Visual Scene Graph (VSG)** | Vision / AI | A structural representation of an image that organizes detected objects, their spatial coordinates, material properties, and semantic relationships into a directed graph. |
| **Cross-modal Alignment** | Multimodal AI | The technique of projecting data from different modalities (e.g., vision, audio, text) into a shared vector space where semantic distance corresponds to physical and contextual similarity. |
| **Binaural Audio** | Spatial Audio | A method of recording or synthesizing audio that uses two microphones (or simulation models) configured to replicate the spatial sound fields heard by a human. |
| **Soundscape** | Acoustics | The acoustic environment of a space as perceived by a listener, consisting of continuous hums (geophony/anthrophony) and discrete events. |
| **Reverberation Time (RT60)** | Acoustics | The time required for the sound pressure level to decrease by 60 decibels after the sound source has ceased. It is highly dependent on room volume and wall materials. |
| **Psychoacoustic Masking** | Psychoacoustics | The phenomenon where the perception of one sound is affected or blocked by the presence of another sound (e.g., loud wind masking distant footsteps). |
| **Active Resonator** | Core Theme | An object in a scene that actively generates sound energy (e.g., a motor, running water, vocal cords). |
| **Passive Resonator** | Core Theme | An object in a scene that does not generate sound itself but vibrates or reflects sound when hit by acoustic energy (e.g., a hollow drum, concrete walls). |
